Starting weights
Week 1 Block 1 — build
Block 1 (weeks 1–8): Volume-focused. RPE 7–8 on main lifts. Add 5 lbs/week to bench, 5–10 lbs/week to squat and deadlift. Do not ego lift in weeks 1–3 — this is intentional. Building work capacity and re-establishing movement patterns after detraining.
Deload (week 9): Drop all working weights by 40%. Cut total sets by half. Same exercises, same rest periods — just lighter and fewer sets. You will feel ready to train heavy. Resist it. This is where gains are locked in.
Block 2 (weeks 10–16): Intensity-focused. RPE 8–9 on main lifts. Volume drops slightly but weight increases significantly. Training closer to failure. Only add weight if the previous week felt at or below RPE 8.

RPE 7 — could do 3 more reps

RPE 9 — could do 1 more rep

General warmup (5 min before every session): 5 min stationary bike at easy pace to raise core temp and lubricate joints. Not optional — your connective tissue needs it especially on leg days and after cold mornings at Boulder altitude.
Push day warmup
Band pull-aparts: 2×20
Wall slides: 2×10
Empty bar bench: 2×10
50% working weight: 1×8
75% working weight: 1×4
Then begin working sets
Pull day warmup

Dead hangs: 3×20 sec
Band face pulls: 2×15
Light lat pulldown: 1×12 at 50%
Then begin working sets
Leg day warmup
Hip circles: 10 each direction
Leg swings: 10 each leg
BW squat: 2×15 controlled
Empty bar squat: 2×8
50% working weight: 1×6
75% working weight: 1×3
Shoulder note (swimmer history)
Always do band pull-aparts before pushing
No overhead barbell press — use landmine or DB
If shoulder aches on bench: widen grip slightly
Face pulls are non-negotiable on push days
Daily protein target: 155–175g. At 155 lbs building muscle with 2x/day training, this is the floor. Your smoothie should be 40–50g alone. Track loosely for the first 2 weeks so you know where you actually stand.
